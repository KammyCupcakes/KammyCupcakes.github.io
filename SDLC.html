The software development life cycle (SDLC) is a process used for structuring the development of any software system, from initiation through to implementation. SDLC is very useful when trying to organize the process into a series of steps. This cycle ends with the fulfillment of all requirements. The software development life cycle comprises of seven distinct phases:


SDLC's Roles
Planning Product Manager and Business Sponsor (Identification of the system for development)
Analysis Business Analyst and Product Manager (Performing a detailed analysis)
Design UI Designers (Designing the system model)
Development Front and Back End Developers (Development of database and code)
Testng Usability Tester and Product Manager (Writing and Executing test cases)
Deployment Release Manager and Product Manager (Delivery of product)
Maintenance System Manager (System changes and adjustment)
